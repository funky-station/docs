## Overview

I want to port a antagonist i made on DeltaV called hitman. Hitman is a subgamemode (theres a midround variant) solo antagonist that hyperfocuses on a single kill.

## Background

I orginally thought of this antag on funky as a kill objective only killer with a faction system https://github.com/funky-station/docs/pull/59. After a while and actually coding it the idea has changed massively Thusly, why this is a separate proposal.

## Features to be added
- Hitman subgamemode and shuttle based ghost role variant.

### hitman objectives 
Hitman objectives are the normal escape to cc objective and a teach a lesson objective(though if requested, this can be a kill objective). Alongside this are two "modifer" objectives. Modifier objectives are untracked objectives that provide a limitation or needed action for the kill. As examples, the current list of provided modifer objectives are listed below.
the current list of objectives is as follows:
- Make their death look like an accident
- Kill them in a public space
- Kill them dramatically
- Draw-out the kill
- Inform them about the assassination long before it happens
- Kill them cleanly
- Kill them in a funny way
- Kill them indirectly
- Kill them nicely
- Kill someone close to them and make sure they know
- Gain their trust

Generally, all modifier objectives should push more towards free from as to encourage creativity and stop objectives from basically requiring one particular item or action.

### contract system
a ability they have which would pull up a ui of their current targets and their modifers. would allow them to complete these sets of objectives and get a new set of objectives. this acts as a way to ensure the hitman player has something to do if they end up killing their target too soon.

## Game Design Rationale
### Seriously Silly
while mostly serious, Several of the objectives encourage setting up absurd kills. this should provide for silly enough outcomes.
### There is no Winning or Losing
as noted above, the primary engagement of hitman is through planning out their kill. the nature of hitman as having free rp objectives focuses them away from just careing about green texing.
### Take Things Slow
the nature of this antag is planning, thusly its focusing the player towards a slower thought out playstyle. it encourages the player to take their time by not implicitly rushing them with anything else.
### Maximizing Roleplay Potential
many objectives encourage interacting with the person(gain their trust, inform them, dramatic, nicely, and kill someone close) while most encourage taking time to investagate the person as to prepare for the kill. interacting with your target and thusly roleplaying with them is highly encouraged.
### Dynamic Environment
objectives like accidental and indirect encourage using the enviroment around you to do the kill. beyond that, half the point is setting up wild kill methods so using the enviroument is a must.

## Roundflow & Player interaction
From what ive seen, hitman generaly comes into play near the latter half of the round, introducing a new threat for secuirty to interact with and new even fro everyone else to react to for some variants. Basically, it spices up the latter half of a round.

Generally speaking, my intention is simple: take a lot of time planning out a kill and then do so to success or failure. its only enforced through objectives which is to say not really. but someone who does ignore the objectives will not have much ability to grief due to the lack of fire rate and ammo the kits provide. if someone ignores the modifers it comes out to being a tol or at worst simple kill objective, which means those who dont play as intended do not cause much harm. 


## Administrative & Server Rule Impact (if applicable)
the only new issues would be people complaining a hitman did not follow their modifers. though this requires the given player to actual know that they are not doing such, and thusly should be very limited. the mechanics ensure that the likelhood there is something new to complain about is limited due to the lack of information. Griefing-wise the above part about intended play covers it. 

# Technical Considerations
the only truly new elements are the card implant and contract system, which both should not cause anything preformance wise.

the contract system would need a ui, the ui would likely be a basic box with drop down menus in a list for each contract, showing their target and modifiers. each contract having buttons for marking it as complete and if they failed objectives. a button at the bottom would be there to get a new contract once the current one is complete and goes on a long cooldown to ensure there is no spamming.
