Scrambleking	ℹ️ Open PR	https://github.com/funky-station/funky-station/pull/2953


# Overview
This proposal recomends flatpack (machine/research) removal, and reducing flatpack use in pre-mapped flatpacks.


# Background
Flatpacks have become a staple of science, simply slip a board in, hit the button, zap it with a yellow box, and its built.
Not only that the machine cheaper than a normal lathe, mobile, and easy to set up.


## The Machine/Research:
Science has lost the feeling of being a tinkering department, and lost the interactions with the greater station as a whole for construction of machines. 
With the removal of flatpacks scientists will actualy leave and interact with the station as a whole, getting access to departments they normaly wouldnt.
You can also work with engineering to have them set up the machines you lathe the parts for should science be lacking, or lone tools out for construction purposes.

## The Mapping level:
While not every flatpack needs to go, there are a fair few that are used as flatpacks that have no reason to be done as such.
If it makes any sense for a prior shift to set it up (EG what we have to do shift start) then it shouldnt be in a flatpack, it should be built.

# Features to be Removed
The Flatpack from regular play (admins could still spawn it if needed)
Mapped flatpack machines (replaced with the actual machines)


# Game Design Rationale

## How does the feature align with our Core Design Principles and game philosphy?

Maintaining Authenticity: It currently takes the same ammount steps to build a machine that can print exotic weaponry as it does to light a cigarette. 7 for the cig, 7 to flatpack and deploy.

Take Things Slow: The flatpack was built for wizden's 60 minuet rounds. We have double lengh. Science also regularly finishes research and has nothing to do. Even when they dont finish research they sometimes have nothing to do.

Maximizing Roleplay Potential (Avoid QOL slop): Actualy interacting with other departments past *throws flatpack out of window* by constructing the machine on site sounds like a hell of a lot more RP to me.

Dynamic Environment: Having people go back and forth with crates and bags to construct things on site is a hell of a lot more dynamic than waiting in line for them to throw a machine at you.

Seriously Silly: Poping up 30 mounted PKAs or an entire department of machines is funy sure, but it completely fails to be in any way serious.


## Other discussed possiblities:

Rework of the construction system to put in all parts at once, then preform tool tasks on the "in progress" sprite
While this would be ideal, it can be done simultaiously outside of this PR and does not fit within it's scope.

Assign a T3 research slot to the flatpack and increase the cost of the machine
While useful, the only slot that the flatpack would accurately fit is industrial, which is already one of the more picked catagories. 
It may be possible to theme it for experimental, which is sorely lacking in useful T3, but there is curently too large of a disparity in theme currently.

Increase cost of using the flatpack
A cost increase on the flatpack (proposed at 200%) has been suggested, but it fails to accurately hit the problems with the flatpack.
Additionaly hyperlathes are already cheaper than the flatpack, but are not utilised in it's place.


## Arguments against change (from real people):

All flatpacking does is make you able to pop up any machine anywhere. Unless your looking to just summon a mounted PKA wall out of nothing or something, this has no benefit.

Over half of science is antags every shift, nothing would get done
If this is true we need to seperately look at why so many antags are chosing science and fix that. Doing so is outside of the scope of this PR.

Engineering would be lost! How would we move machines that need flatpacks around! (creative liberties taken from "just remove engineering while you are at it IG")
Most of the machines that use flatpacks could just be given a deconstruction, or recipie with time. Worst case you can unwrench and fulerton it for long distances.
